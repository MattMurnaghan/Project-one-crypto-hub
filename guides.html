How does crypto work?

The "Blockchain"

Before getting into the crypto space, there are a few things you should know:

  • All cryptocurrencies are created using blockchain technology. A blockchain is like a shared ledger, that works the same way a normal ledger would, but with a brilliant difference.
  • A normal ledger's job is to hold a record of transactions, classically used in banks to record the flow of money. A blockchain acts like a distributed ledger, where every point in the chain holds a complete record of every transaction that has ever occured on the chain.
  • Every point in the chain, (every ledger) is protected with cryptography (special codes) so that no one can tamper with the records on the chain.
  • If someone does manage to alter a node and break the rules, it doesn't matter, because when the node is checked by the next point on the chain, it's ledger won't be copied as it didnt follow the rules!
  • This way, no one can ever tamper with the ledger and full trust is guaranteed without the need for a third party!

How can I buy it?

Here are some of the ways that you can purchase cryptocurrencies:

    Financial Apps

  • Apps like PayPal or Venmo in the US, allow users to purchase cryptocurrency directly on the platform.

    • Crypto Exchanges

  • Exchanges like Binance and Coinbase offer a brilliant begginner option for those just starting out in the crypto space.
  • With low costs, wide selections of currencies and the option to store directly on the exchange, they are a very popular choice.

    • Trading Apps

  • Trading apps like Robinhood and Webull allow you to purchase select cryptos on their platform with no direct comissions on sales.
  • However, they do charge a spread markup on purchases, to what extent is unclear as they have yet to reveal this information.

    • Traditional Brokers

  • Some traditional brokers are now giving their users the ability to trade in cryptocurrencies.
  • This essentially works the same way that a broker would manage a standard stock portfolio.

    • Bitcoin ATMs

  • Bitcoin ATMs are a less common way to purchase purely bitcoin.
  • They are not linked to any other currencies and are a simple way for people to get involved in the space.
