Glossary - Market Terminology

Your crypto definition guide

  • There are a lot of terms to come to grips with when talking about investing, especially in the crypto-space
  • We have compiled a list of terms that are common when discussing investment opportunites and attempting to analyse the behaviour of the crypto market-space
  • Click the arrow at the end of the definition boxes below to see an in depth explanation of each term:

Bull markets & Bear markets

Bull Market
A market in which share prices are rising, encouraging buying
Bear Market
A market in which share prices are falling, encouraging selling

Market Cap, Crypto Exchange & Wallets

Market cap (Market capitilisation):
when talking about crypto, it is the value of a crypto project, calculated by multiplying the total number of coins that have been generated by the price of a single coin at any given time.
Crypto Exchange
A digital market place where cryptocurrency can be bought and sold.
Wallet
A tool used for accessing cryptocurrency addresses on a blockchain. They hold the crypto address, and the private and public keys needed to access it.

Bitcoin, Altcoin, Smart contracts

Bitcoin:
The first and most valuable cryptocurrency. To date, it has priced as high as $60,000.
Altcoin:
Any coin/cryptocurrency that is not Bitcoin. This can range from some of the most popular coins out there like Ethereum, to some of the much more minimal and unstable cryptoscurrencies like Dogecoin.
Smart contracts
An algorithmic program that enacts the terms of a contract automatically based on its code. One of the main value propositions of the Ethereum network is its ability to execute smart contracts.

Mining, Blocks & Blockchain

Mining:
The method by which Bitcoin and other currencies are generated and the transactions involving new coins are verified. It involves complicated cryptographic puzzles being verified and is the basis by which blockchain technology functions.
Block:
A block denotes a collection of data on the blockchain. When talking about cryptocurrency, these blocks hold transaction records for users on the chain. Once a block becomes full, a new block is created to keep the chain going.
Blockchain:
The full record of all blocks, ordered sequentially, creating an unchangeable ledger by which all other transactions are verified.

Decentralisation, DeFi & DApps

Decentralisation:
The principle of distributing power away from a central point. Blockchains are traditionally decentralized because they require majority approval from all users to operate and make changes, rather than a central authority.
DeFi (Decentralised Finance):
Financial activities conducted without the involvement of an intermediary, like a bank, government, or other financial institution.
DApps (Decentralised Applications):
Applications created by developers, usually using blockchain technology, that don't require any governing body to complete actions. DeFi activities are often carried out using smart contracts, constructed from a number of DApps.

Coins, ICOs & Tokens

Coin:
A representative store of digital value that lives on a given blockchain or cryptocurrency network. Some blockchains have the same name for both the network and the coin, like Bitcoin. Others can have different names for each, like the Stellar blockchain, which has a native coin called Lumen.
ICO (Initial Coin Offering):
The first time a coin is released to market and a way of raising funds for a new crypto project. ICOs are similar to Initial Public Offerings (IPOs) of stocks.
Tokens:
Tokens exist in a digital record on the blockchain. But tokens aren't money, as money is typically understood. Instead, they represent things. Often a digital token represents physical or intellectual property, such as a work of art, a piece of music or a book. The best-known example of this is the non-fungible token or NFT.
